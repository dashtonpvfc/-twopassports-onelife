Woke on our last day on the beach to blazing sunshine and clear blue skies - first time we have unbroken sky since Chiang Mai in the North. Unbelievable. Had breakfast and booked Bolt for the 30 minute drive inland to the old town.

![](krabioldtown1%20(1).jpg)

![](krabioldtown1%20(3).jpg)

Weather is unbelievable - the place looks completely different in sunlight. Oh well, try again very soon hopefully. Krabi Seabass hotel is absolutely beautiful to soften the blow.

![](krabioldtown1%20(4).jpg)

![](krabioldtown1%20(5).jpg)

Chilled around the pool, swam, and had a couple of Changs. Mel had a good sleep in the lounger.

![](krabioldtown1%20(6).jpg)

![](krabioldtown1%20(7).jpg)

Walked into the old town early doors, lovely area along the river - the Thai's make a great effort for Christmas..puts the cesspit that is the UK currently to shame - in many ways.

![](krabioldtown1%20(8).jpg)

![](krabioldtown1%20(9).jpg)

![](krabioldtown1%20(10).jpg)

Saw a double rainbow and found an old craft beer joint with a random ale on tap which was very good - Mel had a chicken rice snack which was good.

![](krabioldtown1%20(11).jpg)

![](krabioldtown1%20(12).jpg)

Then walked to a lovely quirky bar and met the owner and a bloke from Manchester who is just dossing there for 8 months to avoid going back to Didsbury. Really funny and genuine people!

![](krabioldtown1%20(13).jpg)

![](krabioldtown1%20(14).jpg)

![](krabioldtown1%20(15).jpg)

We then dug out an Italian restaurant where Mel had a carbonara and I had the spiciest pizza on the menu - family size naturally and covered it in chilli flakes and Tabasco sauce. Washed it down with a Hofbrau house German beer - it was all lovely.

![](krabioldtown1%20(16).jpg)

![](krabioldtown1%20(17).jpg)

![](krabioldtown1%20(18).jpg)

![](krabioldtown1%20(19).jpg)

Walked back along the river and the bustling night market and was in bed at about 9:30 as we have to up at 6am for an early flight to Bangkok.
